Articles of Pacification

In the name of God, Amen. Whereas Captain Cudjoe, Captain Accompong, Captain Johnny, Captain Cuffee, Captain Quaco... have been in a state of war and hostility... they mutually, sincerely, and amicably, have agreed to the following articles:
  • I. That all hostilities shall cease on both sides for ever.
  • II. That the said Captain Cudjoe, the rest of his captains, adherents, and men shall be for ever hereafter in a perfect state of freedom and liberty, excepting those produced by the treaty...
  • III. That they shall enjoy and possess, for themselves and posterity for ever, all the lands situate and lying between Trelawney Town and the Cockpits, to the amount of fifteen hundred acres.
  • IV. That they shall have liberty to plant the said lands with coffee, cocoa, ginger, tobacco, and cotton, and to breed cattle, hogs, goats, or any other stock, and dispose of the produce.
  • V. That Captain Cudjoe, and all the Captain's adherents, shall all live together within the bounds of Trelawney Town, and they have liberty to hunt where they shall think fit.
  • VI. That the said Captain Cudjoe, and his successors, do use their best endeavours to take, kill, suppress, or destroy... all rebels wheresoever they be, throughout this island.
  • VII. That in case this island be invaded by any foreign enemy, the said Captain Cudjoe... shall then, upon notice given, immediately repair to any place the Governor shall appoint...
  • VIII. That if any white man shall do any manner of injury to Captain Cudjoe... they shall apply to any commanding officer or magistrate... for justice.
  • IX. That if any negroes shall hereafter run away from their masters or owners, and shall fall into Captain Cudjoe's hands, they shall immediately be sent back...
  • X. That all negroes taken, since the raising of this party by Captain Cudjoe's people, shall immediately be returned.
  • XI. That Captain Cudjoe, and his successors, shall wait on his Excellency, or the Commander in Chief for the time being, every year, if thereunto required.
  • XII. That Captain Cudjoe... shall have full power to inflict any punishment they think proper for crimes committed by their men among themselves, death only excepted.
  • XIII. That Captain Cudjoe... shall cut, clear, and keep open, large and convenient roads from Trelawney Town to Westmorland and St. James's.
  • XIV. That two white men... shall constantly live and reside with Captain Cudjoe... in order to maintain a friendly correspondence.
  • XV. That Captain Cudjoe shall, during his life, be Chief Commander in Trelawney Town; after his decease the command to devolve on his brother, Captain Accompong...
Done at the Camp near Trelawney Town, March 1, 1738.
Source: R.C. Dallas, The History of the Maroons
