About the Research

Correcting the Record. Honoring the Ancestors.

40 Years of Discovery

This website is the culmination of over four decades of meticulous research into the history of the Trelawny Town Maroons. For too long, the narrative of the Maroons has been told through the eyes of their oppressorsâ€”colonial governors, British military officers, and plantation owners.

Our mission has been to peel back the layers of colonial bias to reveal the truth about a free people who maintained their sovereignty in the face of the British Empire. We have scoured archives in Jamaica, Nova Scotia, Sierra Leone, and London, piecing together the fragmented story of the 1795 War and the subsequent betrayal that led to the exile of an entire community.

Why This Matters

The story of Trelawny Town is not just Jamaican history; it is a global story of resistance, diplomacy, and survival. The Trelawny Maroons were not "savages" as depicted in colonial logs, but sophisticated strategists who fought a superpower to a standstill.

By digitizing these records and presenting this comprehensive history, we aim to restore the dignity of Captain Leonard Parkinson, Montague James, and the hundreds of men, women, and children who were forcibly removed from their beloved Cockpit Country.

"We speak for those whose voices were silenced by the wind and the waves. We remember Trelawny Town."
