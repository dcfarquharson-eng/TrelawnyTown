Articles of Pacification (1738)

In the name of God, Amen. Whereas Captain Cudjoe, Captain Accompong, Captain Johnny, Captain Cuffee, Captain Quaco... have been in a state of war and hostility... they mutually, sincerely, and amicably, have agreed to the following articles:
  • I. That all hostilities shall cease on both sides for ever.
  • II. That the said Captain Cudjoe, the rest of his captains, adherents, and men shall be for ever hereafter in a perfect state of freedom and liberty.
  • III. That they shall enjoy and possess, for themselves and posterity for ever, all the lands situate and lying between Trelawney Town and the Cockpits, to the amount of fifteen hundred acres.
  • IV. That they shall have liberty to plant the said lands with coffee, cocoa, ginger, tobacco, and cotton, and to breed cattle, hogs, goats, or any other stock.
  • V. That Captain Cudjoe, and all the Captain's adherents, shall all live together within the bounds of Trelawney Town, and hunt where they think fit.
  • VI. That the said Captain Cudjoe, and his successors, do use their best endeavours to take, kill, suppress, or destroy all rebels wheresoever they be.
  • VII. That in case this island be invaded by any foreign enemy, the said Captain Cudjoe shall immediately repair to any place the Governor shall appoint to repel invaders.
  • VIII. That if any white man shall do injury to Captain Cudjoe or his people, they shall apply to any magistrate for justice; and vice versa.
  • IX. That if any negroes shall hereafter run away from their masters and fall into Captain Cudjoe's hands, they shall immediately be sent back.
  • X. That all negroes taken, since the raising of this party by Captain Cudjoe's people, shall immediately be returned.
  • XI. That Captain Cudjoe, and his successors, shall wait on his Excellency, or the Commander in Chief for the time being, every year.
  • XII. That Captain Cudjoe shall have full power to inflict punishment for crimes committed by his men, death only excepted.
  • XIII. That Captain Cudjoe shall cut, clear, and keep open large and convenient roads from Trelawney Town to Westmorland and St. James's.
  • XIV. That two white men shall constantly live and reside with Captain Cudjoe and his successors to maintain friendly correspondence.
  • XV. That Captain Cudjoe shall be Chief Commander in Trelawney Town; succeeded by Accompong, Johnny, Cuffee, Quaco, and then by Governor's appointment.
Signatures, this 1st day of March, 1738.
Cudjoe X his mark
